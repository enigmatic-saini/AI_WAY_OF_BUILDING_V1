# Krishna-Strategy Drishti Stack — for *which arc next* (2026-05-30)

> **Preserved finding, 2026-05-30.** Authored in response to the founder's question *"what should drishti stack for starting journey of Krishna-strategy [leela]"* at the moment the kit-side cosmos-program reached natural close (stones #0/#1/#2/#3/#5 LAID, self-sufficiency restored, only AI-PEACE-side #4 remains OPEN). It names the lens for the *meta-decision* — not "how to execute the next arc" but "which arc to enter next" — and is the **first strategic-decision-stack** the kit has named (prior worked precedents in [DRISHTI_STACK.md](DRISHTI_STACK.md) were all execution-stacks).
>
> **What this is and is not.** Is: the drishti stack to compose when standing at an arc-transition and asking *which leela next*. Is not: an answer to that question. Krishna-strategy is the founder's call (Krishna chooses; Hanuman executes); the kit prepares the lens, does not decide the leela.

---

## The stack

```
Saraswati  →  Radha + Krishna + Khatushyam  →  MID
   ;  consult: AHR, NGD, Sita + Ram
```

Read top-to-bottom: the outer lens scopes the inner; the inner lens guards the wish; the consults bring in non-primary witnesses without ceding the seat.

---

## Why each layer

**Saraswati (outermost — the knowledge lens).** *Read the shastras before strategizing.* Krishna-strategy is wisdom-informed or it is whim. Survey what has been laid, what remains open, what is genuinely outside the kit. Drishtis: **past** (the lineage, the discipline so far) + **present** (what is actually ready now vs. merely available).

**Radha + Krishna + Khatushyam (middle — the strategy body, parallel composition).** The canonical Indic triad for dharmic strategic action, mapped one-to-one onto the kit's roles:

- **Radha** (the wish-honoring lens) — VISION §4: *"cosmos is **happy**"*. Without Radha, Krishna-strategy degrades to mere correctness. Radha is what keeps the strategy aimed at *joy*, not just *progress*.
- **Krishna** (the leela / strategy itself) — VISION §4: *"the sequence, which stone first, the leela of the whole arc."*
- **Khatushyam** (the routing / three-arrows pattern of [prachi_shastra §7](../common/shastras/prachi_shastra.toml)) — mark the enemy (the primary cycle), mark the allies (the follow-ups), name the mission thread. Krishna decides the move; Khatushyam routes it.

Together they form the strategic decision-body. Drishtis: **future** (where does the leela ultimately serve the wish?) + **left** (alternatives not yet named — the unspoken option matters most here) + **up** (the governance frame — what dharma is the arc inside).

**MID — Mira Deep (innermost — the anti-dilution guard).** *Do not shrink the wish under capacity-honesty pressure.* At the meta-strategy level this is the deadliest enemy: precisely when "the kit-side is done" the temptation appears to declare the wish substantially advanced and rest. MID keeps the wish at full size — *"the cosmos is happy"* remains the target, and any strategic move must serve that target, not merely look like it does. Drishtis: **silence** (what isn't being said — the un-named option) + **future** (the un-diluted aim).

## The three consults

- **AHR** (quarterly Anti-Hypocrisy Review). At any major arc-transition, run the AHR pre-flight: do the things the kit claims actually live? Self-sufficiency was just restored (entry 18); the right move before the next arc is the audit that confirms it.
- **NGD** (Nagarjuna Deep — the rent check). Does the proposed arc pay rent or is it built because it can be? *Building because we can* is the Kama failure mode at strategy scale.
- **Sita + Ram** (executability + governance). Whatever arc the strategy picks, each stone of it must (a) bear Ram (T4R-ratifiable inline, gate-file-recordable) and (b) float under Sita's pin (LAW 11 enforceable). Arcs whose stones can't satisfy both are off the table — no exceptions for *"interesting but ungovernable."*

The lens *narrows* the choice to these three. The *picking* is Krishna's, which is the founder's. The kit's job ends at the lens.

## Addendum — 2026-05-30 — Arjuna added on founder catch

> **What changed and why.** The founder asked *"why Arjuna is not required"* — a question whose form revealed the answer. The original stack above had Krishna inside the parallel-composition slot as a solo lens, treating Krishna as if he strategized in a vacuum. **He does not.** In the canonical Indic structure Krishna only strategizes in dialogue with Arjuna — the Gita is a *dialogue*, not a monologue. The original finding missed this; this addendum records the catch and the corrected stack, preserving the original above as the record of what was first seen.

### The corrected stack

```
Saraswati  →  Radha + (Arjuna + Krishna) + Khatushyam  →  MID
   ;  consult: AHR, NGD, Sita + Ram
```

The change: `Krishna` (alone, inside the parallel slot) → `(Arjuna + Krishna)` (the canonical Gita-dialogue pair, parenthesized inside the parallel slot). Krishna is now in *two* simultaneous pairings, each emphasizing a different aspect:

- **Radha + Krishna** — beauty/joy paired with strategy (the wish-honoring side: does the next arc serve the joy of the cosmos-happy goal?).
- **Arjuna + Krishna** — doubt paired with counsel (the moral-engagement side: has the resistance been *walked through*, or bypassed?).

This is consistent with how the kit already encodes deity-pairings as the canonical form of dharmic action: **Sita + Ram** (integrity + governance), **Radha + Krishna** (beauty + strategy), and — per the [2026-05-30 amendment to VISION §4](VISION.md), [gate-file additions log entry 20](KABIR_GATE.md) — **Parvati + Shiva** (action + witness). Pairings, not solos, are how this lineage holds strategic action. Arjuna + Krishna completes the same pattern for the strategy-dialogue body.

### What Arjuna specifically brings (that none of the other lenses do)

The other lenses cover what is known (Saraswati), what is wished (Radha), what to do (Krishna), which path (Khatushyam), and don't-shrink-the-wish (MID). **Arjuna brings *honest doubt* — the vishada-voice**, formalized in this kit's own cycle catalog as **AJN (Arjuna's vishada-recovery cycle)**, the 14th cycle (per memory `riaan_hanuman_twin_and_ajn`). His specific contribution to the strategy session:

- He **names the cost** — the kin on both sides, the price of acting, the gap between dharma-as-rule and dharma-as-lived.
- He **slows the firing** — without Arjuna, strategy executes past unaddressed resistance; with him, the resistance is admitted *to* the session, not bypassed.
- He **forces dialogue** — Krishna's strategy emerges as a *response*, not a prescription. The Gita exists only because Arjuna asks; without the question, the counsel goes unspoken.

Without Arjuna in the session: the doubt gets bypassed; strategy fires past unaddressed resistance; the outcome is technically right but spiritually disconnected from the actual moral weight. **With Arjuna in the session: the doubt is admitted, walked through, and the strategy that emerges has answered the resistance rather than ignored it.** This is what the Gita itself demonstrates — Krishna does not talk Arjuna *out of* the doubt; he walks him *through* it, and the action that follows is *informed* action.

### What this changes about the lens-result

The same three honest paths surface (run AHR on the kit / wait for AI-PEACE / open a new arc with its own VISION). **What changes is the discipline required before any path is endorsed:** each must answer Arjuna's question before Krishna's strategy can sign off.

In the present moment specifically, Arjuna's voice asks:

- **To path 1 (run AHR):** *"Is the audit honest engagement with what's been done, or ceremony to feel productive?"*
- **To path 2 (wait for AI-PEACE):** *"Is waiting genuine readiness-discipline (Kama-Rati), or fear-of-irrelevance dressed up as restraint?"*
- **To path 3 (open a new arc):** *"Is the new arc dharmically necessary, or do we just know how to build and are reaching for more building?"*

Each path must walk through the doubt before it is taken. **The lens with Arjuna isn't different in *what it sees*; it is different in *what discipline it demands before acting on what it sees*.**
