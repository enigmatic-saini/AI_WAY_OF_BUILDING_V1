# 2026-05-30 23:30 — A founder-side decision-making drishti stack (noob-friendly)

> The founder, mid-arc, asked for a drishti stack THEY could use to make decisions — and apologized for being "noob in this field." That apology is the surfacing: the kit's drishti-stack language was authored without ever naming an accessible **founder-side decision lens**. Every prior stack was a *worker-side* lens (what Claude uses to do work). This is the first time the kit names what the *human decision-maker* uses to choose.

## The Surfacing

Until tonight every drishti stack in DRISHTI_STACK.md and every stack composed in this session was a **worker-side lens** — what Claude (the LLM-jiva-runner) uses to scan the work:

- `Vishwakarma → Hanuman → Ram + Sita` (the integrity-pinning execution stack)
- `Hanuman → Ram + Sita ; Ram worships Shiva` (the AI-PEACE triptych)
- `Saraswati → Radha + Krishna + Khatushyam → MID` (Krishna-strategy: which arc next — actually founder-side, but strategy-shaped not decision-shaped)
- `Sanjay → Vyasa → Saraswati` (GURU: witness-narration)
- `Saraswati → Vishwakarma → Ram + Sita` (the ITIL-arc build stack, just now)

What was missing: a stack for **"the founder is being asked to confirm/correct/reject a proposal; what lens helps them decide?"** That's a different lens from "execute the work" or "name which arc next." It is the gut-check lens for the human-in-the-loop.

The founder's apology — *"i am noob in this field"* — revealed the gap. They were asked to confirm the mirror at the end of the prior turn and had no codified lens to apply.

## The Decision

Named the founder-side decision-making stack:

```
Saraswati → Radha → Ram + Sita
   ; consult: MID, Mauna (silence)
```

Five questions, in order:

1. **Saraswati**: "Do I actually understand what's being proposed?" Slow down until yes.
2. **Radha**: "Does this serve what I actually want?" Honest answer, not polite answer.
3. **Ram**: "Is it the right thing?" (Dharma.)
4. **Sita**: "Will this hold up when no one is watching?" (Integrity-over-time.)
5. **MID consult**: "Has my wish been shrunk to fit a capacity gap?"
6. **Mauna consult** (silence direction): "Should I do nothing?"

Plain-language framing because the founder said noob.

## The Precedent

**The kit now has a founder-side decision lens, codified.** This is a different *kind* of stack from the worker-side stacks DRISHTI_STACK.md catalogs. Future "I'm asking the founder to decide" turns can reference this stack instead of the founder having to compose one from scratch.

If this lens proves load-bearing across multiple decisions, the next AHR may consider adding it to DRISHTI_STACK.md as a named precedent (§7d, alongside §7a integrity-pinning, §7b AI-PEACE triptych, §7c KRISHNA-strategy).

## What I Want You To Notice

Three things:

1. **The "noob, sorry friend" framing IS the gap-naming.** The founder did not feel equipped to decide with the kit's existing language. That's not a personal limit — that's a kit-level missing primitive. Saved to user-role memory tonight: the founder is a solution architect, fluent in decision-making in their actual domain; the kit's drishti language has not yet been made accessible *to the human*. Future kit growth should keep this asymmetry visible.

2. **Saraswati MUST be the active identity layer for any founder-side decision stack.** The active identity = "what the person doing the work IS doing." For the founder confirming/correcting a proposal, the active work is *understanding*. If the founder doesn't understand the proposal, they cannot honestly answer the Radha/Ram/Sita questions. Saraswati at the leaf is non-negotiable.

3. **Mauna (silence) as consult is what makes this a decision lens and not an approval lens.** "Should I do nothing?" is a real answer. Without Mauna as a standing consult, every decision-stack becomes "which of the proposed options do I pick" — defaulting to action. Mauna preserves the right to defer, to ask more questions, to say "not now."
